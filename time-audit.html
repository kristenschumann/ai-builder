Automation Opportunities

Ranked by impact: hours reclaimed × alignment to top goal. All estimates are drafts.

#1 · Highest Impact
Student support is handled without me touching it
Medium lift ~3h/week

Students get accurate, warm answers to recording access, cohort switch, and session timing questions within minutes — without me or anyone on my team drafting responses.

I personally respond to 5–10 student emails and Slack messages per day with the same 4–5 answers. One student got 4 separate replies about a cohort switch. Estimated 30–45 min/day of GM time.

n8n workflow monitors the cohort Slack #help channel and a support email address. Incoming message → Claude reads it + classifies the question → pulls the right template answer → sends reply. Cohort switch requests → auto-creates a ticket in Linear/Notion for Ellie or ops to action, sends student a "noted, we'll get you moved" confirmation. Anything outside the known patterns → escalates to human with draft.

#2 · High Impact
Student onboarding and pre-session comms run on autopilot
Quick win ~2h/week

Every student gets perfectly timed, personalized pre-session reminders, setup instructions, and post-session recording links — without manual sending or forwarding.

HubSpot test sends get forwarded manually to the team. Recording links are referenced verbally. Setup instructions are repeated in Slack and email responses. Multiple email exchanges are triggered by information gaps that a good onboarding sequence would close.

HubSpot sequences fully built out: enrollment → welcome → pre-session (24h before) → post-session recording link (auto-triggered by Zoom webhook). Slack Slackbot welcome message auto-answers top 5 questions on join. I review and approve templates once; the system runs every cohort.

#3 · High Impact
Instructor coordination has a clear status system, not an email thread
Medium lift ~2h/week

GRE coaches, VC course partners, and TAs always know exactly what's pending, what's been reviewed, and what's next — without long email threads and me as the tracking system.

GRE slide review thread has 10+ emails over 5+ weeks (Eric, Matt, Bruce). The VC course partner thread started in January and is still open. I'm the only one tracking status, and it leaks into my inbox constantly.

Notion database for each instructor: one row per deliverable, with status (awaiting slides, in review, feedback sent, approved, recorded). Claude Code generates a weekly digest of what's blocked and what's overdue. Auto-email to instructors when status changes. I set the status; the system nags on my behalf.

#4 · Medium Impact
L3–L5 design gets protected time with a weekly forcing function
Quick win ~3h/week freed up

L3–L5 curriculum gets 3+ dedicated hours per week on the calendar, treated as non-negotiable, with a weekly output checkpoint.

L3–L5 received exactly 2 hours of calendar time in a 14-day window. It's being fully displaced by L1/L2 delivery work. Without a hard block, it won't happen until L1/L2 delivery pressure drops — which won't be until after L3–L5 was due.

Recurring 3h block on calendar every Tuesday or Wednesday, marked "L3–L5 Design" — immovable. Paired with a weekly Claude Code session to run the `/session-slide-outline` and `/create-session-guide` pipeline. Scheduled reminder to Nick each Friday with 1-sentence L3 progress update.

#5 · Medium Impact
Session debrief insights are captured and actioned without a meeting
Medium lift ~1.5h/week

Post-session insights (what worked, what to change, student questions) are captured automatically from Zoom recordings and converted into action items — without a live debrief meeting after every session.

The calendar has a 30-min "L1 Debrief Hold" blocked after every single L1 session (4 in this window). That's 2h of live debrief time. The meeting captures patterns that a Zoom transcript + Claude analysis could surface faster.

Zoom recording → Granola transcript → Claude reads it → generates structured debrief doc (top student questions, confusion points, energy moments, suggested changes for next session) → drops into Notion session folder. Debrief hold becomes async: I review the Claude doc in 10 minutes instead of a 30-min live call.

#6 · Quick Win
Revenue pulse is visible without pulling it
Quick win ~1h/week

Every Monday morning I see: enrollment count vs. target, revenue vs. $209k, pacing to hit the goal, and top 3 bottlenecks — without pulling any data manually.

No data source in the 14-day window showed revenue tracking or enrollment dashboards. The $209k goal is the #1 metric, but there's no visible system for monitoring pacing or spotting shortfalls early.
